## *The Basecamp* — Launch Campaign

---

## 1. Campaign Objective

Drive 20 confirmed bookings and 10+ five-star reviews within 90 days of launch by positioning *The Basecamp* as the obvious alternative to generic hotel blocks for design-conscious families and friend groups visiting Denver.

---

## 2. Audience Insight

This audience isn't choosing between hotels and Airbnb. They're choosing between a forgettable trip and a memorable one. They plan together in group chats, share itineraries on Instagram Stories, and make decisions based on what the house *looks like in photos* before they look at the price. They want to feel like insiders, not tourists — and they're willing to pay a modest premium for a space that feels intentional. The pain point they've lived before: splitting a generic suburban rental that looked great online and felt hollow in person. What they're actually booking is the group photo in front of the fireplace, the kitchen big enough for breakfast together, and the story they get to tell about where they stayed.

---

## 3. Key Message

*The Basecamp* is the Denver home base that makes the whole trip feel worth it — designed with intention, stocked with local knowledge, and sized for the people you actually want to travel with.

**Supporting Messages:**

- **For families:** More space, more privacy, more flexibility than a hotel — without the chaos of figuring out a new city from scratch. (Local guide included.)
- **For friend groups:** A house this well-designed doesn't stay open on ski weekends. Book it before your group chat picks somewhere forgettable.
- **For all guests:** Denver has a new kind of short-term rental. Fewer plastic welcome baskets. More actual taste.

---

## 5. Creative Concept Directions

---

**Concept A: "Your Group Chat Finally Agreed on Something"**

*Headline: Your Group Chat Finally Agreed on Something.*

**Idea:** Lean into the universal chaos of planning a group trip. Content mimics the format of a group text thread — competing opinions, indecision, and then one person drops the listing and everyone goes quiet. Stops the scroll because it's instantly recognizable. Works as a short Reel, a static carousel, or a caption strategy. Tone is dry and warm. The house sells itself once they click through.

**Best for:** Instagram Reels, Facebook Groups, caption voice

---

**Concept B: "Not a Hotel. Not a Random Airbnb."**

*Headline: Not a Hotel. Not a Random Airbnb. Finally.*

**Idea:** Directly names what the audience is tired of without being negative. Positions *The Basecamp* as a third category — the designed, considered, local-insider option that didn't exist before. Visual execution contrasts dull hospitality stock imagery (cold lighting, beige everything) with warm, textured, specific shots of the house. Copy is short and declarative.

**Best for:** Paid social static ads, listing headline, direct message outreach

---

**Concept C: "The House That Knows Denver"**

*Headline: Stay With Someone Who Actually Knows Denver.*

**Idea:** Leads with the local-insider brand value. The host isn't just renting a house — they're handing guests a curated city. Content series features the host's actual Denver recommendations: the breakfast spot worth the line, the ski rental shop that won't waste your morning, the concert pre-game walk. Builds trust and differentiates from faceless property management listings. Functions as both marketing content and a genuine guest resource.

**Best for:** Instagram organic series, listing guidebook copy, influencer brief framing

## 7. Campaign Timeline

---

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–2)
*Get the listing ready to earn trust before it earns traffic.*

- Book and complete professional photography session
- Finalize and publish optimized listing copy on Airbnb and VRBO using Concept B or C voice
- Set up host guidebook with local recommendations (feeds Concept C content)
- Draft Instagram bio, first 6 feed posts, and content calendar
- Conduct warm-network outreach to personal contacts for first 2–3 bookings
- Identify and contact 2 micro-influencer candidates for potential hosted stay

**Gate before Phase 2:** Listing is live, photography is published, at least 1 booking confirmed.

---

### Phase 2: Launch & Amplify (Weeks 3–8)
*Get eyes on the listing. Convert traffic into bookings and early reviews.*

- Activate paid Instagram promotion ($400 spend, starting with top-performing organic post)
- Post in 3–5 relevant Facebook groups using organic, community-first voice
- Execute influencer hosted stay (if confirmed) — brief them on Concepts A or C
- Post 3x per week on Instagram: mix of house beauty shots, local tips, and social-proof content
- Collect and respond to all early reviews publicly and warmly
- Monitor listing views and booking conversion weekly; adjust pricing or photography if conversion lags

**Gate before Phase 3:** 10+ bookings confirmed, first 5 reviews posted.

---

### Phase 3: Optimize & Sustain (Weeks 9–12)
*Use what's working. Protect the review trajectory.*

- Deploy $200 reserve budget to boost highest-performing paid post or retarget warm audiences
- Audit listing copy using language from guest reviews — update headline and description to reflect what guests actually valued
- Reach out to all completed-stay guests with a personal message requesting a review (within Airbnb's messaging guidelines)
- Document what drove the most bookings (channel, creative concept, time of week posted) for ongoing strategy
- Begin building an email or repeat-guest touchpoint for future direct bookings

**End-of-campaign deliverable:** 1-page performance read summarizing what drove bookings, which creative concept resonated, and where to put the next $1,500.
