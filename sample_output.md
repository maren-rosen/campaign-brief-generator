## 1. Campaign Objective

Drive 20 confirmed bookings and 10+ five-star reviews within 90 days of Airbnb/VRBO listing launch by positioning this property as the design-forward Denver basecamp that makes the whole trip feel intentional.

---

## 2. Audience Insight

This audience isn't just booking a place to sleep — they're managing logistics for a group. Whether it's four friends chasing a powder day or a family making memories at Red Rocks, the person doing the booking carries the invisible pressure of *making it good for everyone.* They're scrolling late at night, comparing photos, doing the mental math of "is this worth splitting?" They choose a property the same way they choose a restaurant: it has to feel right before they even read the details.

**What they actually care about:**
- **The group experience.** Enough space to gather without tripping over each other. A kitchen that actually works. A living room worth sitting in.
- **Proximity without isolation.** Close to the action (ski resorts, venues, downtown Denver) but not a hotel corridor.
- **Bragging rights.** They want to text the group chat a photo of the space that makes everyone say *yes.*
- **Frictionless trust.** Great photos, fast responses, clear reviews. Decision anxiety is real at this price point.
- **Value clarity.** $X split 6 ways hits differently than a hotel block — but only if they can see it.

---

## 3. Key Message

**This is the house that makes your Denver trip a story worth telling.**

**Supporting Messages:**

1. **Design that earns its keep.** Every detail was considered — not for the 'gram, but because beautiful spaces change how a group feels together.

2. **Your mountain basecamp, five minutes from everything.** Ski gear, concert wristbands, coffee — you're close to all of it without being in the middle of any of it.

3. **Six people, one roof, zero compromises.** Room to spread out, sleep well, cook a real breakfast, and actually want to hang out with each other the next morning.

## 5. Creative Concept Directions

---

### Concept A: *"The Group Chat Approved"*
**Headline:** The house that made everyone agree for once.

**Direction:** Leans into the universal truth of group travel — someone always has a complaint, except this time. Creative uses split-screen or carousel formats showing the moment of group arrival vs. the chaos of a cramped hotel. Copy is playful, self-aware, slightly cheeky. Speaks directly to the *booker* who is tired of being the one who picked wrong. Works well in paid social and listing headline copy.

---

### Concept B: *"Base Camp, Elevated"*
**Headline:** The mountain is calling. This is where you stay before you answer.

**Direction:** Leans aspirational and sensory. Cinematic photography of the space at golden hour, ski gear propped by a beautifully designed entryway, steam rising from a coffee mug. No people required — the space does the talking. Speaks to the feeling of *the night before the adventure* — warm, anticipatory, a little romantic about travel. Works best as Instagram feed content, listing hero image, and creator content brief.

---

### Concept C: *"Denver, Like a Local. Comfort, Like You Designed It."*
**Headline:** The insider address your hotel tab could never afford.

**Direction:** Positions the property as the insider's alternative — not just cheaper than hotels when split, but *better.* Creative features a curated local guide (downloadable or listing-linked): best coffee near the slopes, pre-concert dinner spots, where to rent gear. The house is the anchor; local knowledge is the value-add. Speaks to experienced travelers who know hotel lobbies are dead time. Works well as organic content, email nurture, and listing description framing.
